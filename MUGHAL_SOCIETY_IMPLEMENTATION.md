### Comprehensive Coverage Areas:

#### 1. **Religious Movements: Bhakti & Sikhism**
- Bhakti movement core principles (personal devotion over rituals)
- Key saints: Kabir, Guru Nanak, Mirabai
- Sikhism evolution: peaceful community ‚Üí militarized Khalsa (1699)
- Response to persecution, 5 Ks, Guru Gobind Singh transformation
- Social-spiritual revolution through accessibility & equality messaging

#### 2. **Art & Architecture: The Indo-Islamic Synthesis**
- Persian elements (arches, domes, minarets, calligraphy)
- Indian elements (stone decoration, pietra dura, jali screens)
- Key monuments: Taj Mahal, Red Fort, Agra Fort, Fatehpur Sikri, Lahore Fort
- Charbagh gardens (four-quadrant water design)
- Mughal painting: Indo-Persian miniatures, nature studies under Jahangir
- Innovation: unique style appealing to both Muslim & Hindu populations

#### 3. **Literature: Court Persian vs. Regional Vernacular**
- Elite culture: Persian court histories (Akbarnama), poetry (Ghazals)
- Popular culture: Bhakti saints deliberately chose regional languages
- **Tulsidas & Ramcharitmanas** (Awadhi): Made Ramayana accessible
- **Surdas & Sursagar** (Braj Bhasha): Devotional poetry establishing literary language
- **Mirabai**: Devotional poems transcending gender & caste
- Impact: Elevated Hindi, Bengali, Marathi from dialects to prestigious literary mediums
- Language split: Elite (Persian-Sanskrit) vs. Masses (regional)

#### 4. **Economic Structure: Agriculture, Villages & Trade**
- Zabt revenue system: land measurement, tax standardization, cash income
- Village economy: Jajmani system (caste-based, non-cash barter exchange)
- Stability provided but reinforced caste hierarchy
- Internal trade: spices, textiles, metalwork, gems
- **European arrival**: Portuguese, Dutch, English, French trading companies
- Shift from Indian merchant control ‚Üí European monopoly
- Coastal factories: initial trade posts ‚Üí military bases ‚Üí territorial conquest
- Long-term: European dominance in global trade, pathway to colonialism
